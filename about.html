A croissant is a French pastry, shaped like a crescent moon, that is made from a laminated yeast dough. It's known for its flaky and buttery texture, achieved through a process of layering butter into the dough. Croissants are typically eaten for breakfast or as a snack. Laminated Dough: The defining characteristic of a croissant is its laminated dough. This involves repeatedly rolling and folding the dough with layers of butter, creating multiple thin layers that result in a flaky texture when baked. Yeast-Leavened: Unlike puff pastry, croissant dough contains yeast, which contributes to its lighter, airy texture. The croissant as we know it today was popularized in France in the 19th century.
